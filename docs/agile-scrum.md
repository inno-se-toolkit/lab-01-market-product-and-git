## Scrum Roles
- Product Owner: Responsible for the product vision and prioritizing the backlog (e.g., deciding which features like "Stories" or "Wallet" to build next).
- Scrum Master: Ensures the team follows Scrum practices, removes blockers, and facilitates meetings.
- Development Team: Cross-functional group (Back-end, Mobile, QA, DevOps) that builds the actual product.

## Scrum Rituals
- Sprint: A 2-week development cycle to deliver a functional increment (e.g., a new update).
- **Sprint Planning: Meeting at the start of the sprint to define the goal and select tasks from the backlog.
- **Daily Standup: A quick 15-minute daily sync to discuss progress and obstacles.
- **Sprint Review: A demo session at the end of the sprint to show what was built to stakeholders.
- **Sprint Retrospective: A meeting to discuss what went well and what could be improved in the team's workflow.

## Scrum Artifacts
- Product Backlog: A prioritized list of all features and requirements for Telegram.
- Sprint Backlog: The set of tasks selected for the current 2-week sprint.
- Increment: The final, tested version of the software released at the end of a sprint.
